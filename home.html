Fontaines D.C. is one of the newest bands that has had a great breakthrough in popularity - combining aesthetics of rock,
post-punk and even club music it is truly a breath of fresh air in the music scene. The band, consisting of Grian Chatten
(vocals), Carlos O'Connell (guitar), Conor Curley (guitar), Conor Deegan III (bass), and Tom Coll (drums) has a style
of instrumentation and specific vocal use, as well as unique, almost poetic lyrics to some of their songs, that harkens back
to the great post-punk bands of old - mainly Joy Division. Their songs have great variation among them - loud or
melancholic, fast or slow in tempo, having great interplay among the more traditional sounding electric guitars and drums
or employing synthesizers in their soon to be released album "Skinty Fia".


In their debut album's first song - "Big" the leading man Grian Chatten expresses himself
thusly: "My childhood while small but I'm gonna be big". However crude their first album
was (which should be expected for a band that plays music in their genre) these words might
still come true - their second album, "A Hero's Death" was nominated for album of the year
at the Grammy awards and thus far has been their highest achievement.
